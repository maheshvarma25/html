Apollo 111 was the spaceflight that landed the first humans, Americans Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin, on the Moon on
july 20, 1969, at 20:18 UTC. Armstrong became the first to step onto the lunar surface 6 hours later on july 21 at
02:56 UTC.

Armstrong spent about three and a halftwo and a half hours outside he spacecraft, Aldrin slighty less; and together they
collected 47.5 pounds(21.5 kg)of lunar material for return to earth. A third member of the mission, Michael Collins, piloted the
command spacecraft alone in lunar orbit until Armstrong and Aldin returned to it for the trip back to earth
